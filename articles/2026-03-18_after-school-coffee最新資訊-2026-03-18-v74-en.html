After School Coffee March 2026 Update: What's New and What's Next

After School Coffee continues to strengthen its position as one of Macau's premier specialty coffee destinations with the release of version 74 (March 2026). This month's updates bring exciting developments in coffee sourcing, quality standards, and customer experience that reflect the growing sophistication of Macau's coffee culture.

New Single-Origin Roasts Arrive This Spring

The highlight of this month's update is the introduction of two carefully selected single-origin coffees from renowned growing regions. After School Coffee has partnered with farmers who prioritize sustainable practices and exceptional quality.

These new offerings align with the specialty coffee movement that has transformed Macau's beverage landscape over the past decade. According to the International Coffee Organization (ICO), global specialty coffee consumption has grown steadily, with Asia-Pacific markets showing particularly strong demand for premium beans.

The first new roast features beans from a Colombian highlands farm at an elevation of 1,800 meters, producing a complex cup with notes of caramel and citrus. The second selection comes from an Ethiopian Yirgacheffe cooperative, known for its bright, floral characteristics that appeal to adventurous coffee enthusiasts.

Commitment to Quality: SCA Standards at the Core

Quality remains the foundation of After School Coffee's operations. The team continues to adhere to the Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) scoring standards, ensuring every cup meets the rigorous 80+ point threshold that defines true specialty coffee.

This commitment extends beyond the final cup to every step of the brewing process. The coffee baristas at After School Coffee follow precise extraction protocols that SCA recommends, maintaining consistency across all service hours. Regular calibration sessions ensure that each cup reflects the beans' optimal flavor profile.

The specialty coffee industry has seen significant evolution in quality standards, with the Coffee Quality Institute (CQI) playing a crucial role in establishing reproducible cupping protocols that roasters and cafes worldwide now follow.

Environmental Initiatives: Sustainability in Practice

March 2026 marks the implementation of new sustainability measures at After School Coffee. The café has transitioned to fully compostable packaging for all takeout orders, aligning with growing consumer expectations for environmentally responsible business practices.

World Coffee Research (WCR) has highlighted the importance of sustainability across the entire coffee supply chain, from farm to cup. After School Coffee's initiatives reflect this holistic understanding, working with suppliers who implement climate-smart agricultural practices.

Additionally, the café has introduced a reusable cup program that offers discounts to customers who bring their own vessels—a small but meaningful step that resonates with Macau's increasingly eco-conscious consumers.

Community Engagement: Coffee Education Sessions

Understanding that informed customers appreciate better coffee, After School Coffee is launching a series of monthly coffee education sessions. These interactive workshops will cover topics ranging from bean selection and brewing techniques to the science behind extraction.

The educational approach mirrors best practices promoted by the Specialty Coffee Association, which emphasizes that consumer knowledge enhances appreciation of quality coffee. Participants will learn about the journey from seed to cup, gaining a deeper understanding of what makes specialty coffee distinct.

These sessions also provide an opportunity for After School Coffee to connect with the local community, building relationships that extend beyond the transactional nature of most café visits.

Technology Integration: Streamlined Ordering Experience

Version 74 introduces enhancements to After School Coffee's digital ordering system. The updated mobile app now features a personalized recommendation engine that suggests drinks based on previous orders and stated preferences.

This technological investment reflects broader trends in the hospitality industry, where data-driven personalization has become essential for customer retention. Practical Tips for Coffee Enthusiasts

  • Ask about origin: Specialty cafés like After School Coffee typically offer detailed information about where their beans come from—take advantage of this knowledge.
  • Try different brewing methods: The same beans can taste remarkably different when prepared as espresso versus pour-over.
  • Store beans properly: Keep coffee in an airtight container away from light and heat to preserve freshness.
  • Visit during off-peak hours: Baristas often have more time to discuss recommendations and answer questions when the café is less busy.
