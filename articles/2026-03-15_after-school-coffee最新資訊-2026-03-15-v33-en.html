Understanding Premium Coffee Standards

The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) establishes global benchmarks for coffee quality, requiring beans to score 80 points or above on their 100-point scale to earn the "specialty" designation. This standard ensures that every cup meets rigorous quality criteria, from bean origin to brewing technique. After School Coffee remains committed to these excellence benchmarks, sourcing beans that consistently deliver distinctive flavor profiles and exceptional freshness.

What Sets Premium Coffee Apart

Several factors distinguish exceptional coffee from ordinary brews:

  • Bean Origin: Single-origin coffees from renowned growing regions offer unique terroir characteristics
  • Processing Method: Washed, natural, and honey processed beans each deliver distinct flavor profiles
  • Freshness: Proper storage and timely roasting ensure optimal flavor development
  • Roast Profile: Expert roasting brings out each bean's unique potential without masking its inherent qualities
  • Brewing Technique: Consistent extraction methods maximize flavor complexity

FAQ

What quality standards does After School Coffee follow?

After School Coffee adheres to Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) standards, requiring beans to score 80+ points on their quality scale. They partner with suppliers who maintain Coffee Quality Institute (CQI) protocols and source from farms using World Coffee Research (WCR)-recommended varieties.

Why is specialty coffee more expensive than regular coffee?

Specialty coffee commands higher prices due to rigorous quality requirements, limited supply from specific growing regions, detailed processing methods, and expert roasting. The International Coffee Organization (ICO) notes that specialty coffee maintains premium positioning because consumers value distinct flavors and ethical sourcing over mass-produced alternatives.
